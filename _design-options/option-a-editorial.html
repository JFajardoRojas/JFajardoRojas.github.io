About

A note on the work

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Colorado School of Mines, working in the Toberer Group. My research sits at the intersection of ML/AI, molecular simulation, and experimental characterization.

I earned a PhD in Materials Science from Mines in 2025 under Diego Gómez-Gualdrón, developing computational workflows to accelerate the discovery of materials for energy applications.

Previously, I received a PhD in (Chemical) Engineering from Universidad de los Andes in Colombia, working with Oscar Alvarez and Diego Pradilla on the experimental characterization of interfacial phenomena.

I envision a future where AI-powered tools unlock data-driven guided experimentation—transforming how we discover solutions for energy and sustainability.
