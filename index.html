<Hello world!> My name is Fernando

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Colorado School of Mines, working in the Toberer Group. I earned a PhD in Materials Science from Mines in 2025 under the guidance of Diego Gómez-Gualdrón, where I developed computational workflows to accelerate the discovery of new materials for energy-related applications. Previously, I received a PhD in Engineering from Universidad de los Andes in Colombia, where I worked with Oscar Alvarez and Diego Pradilla on the experimental characterization of interfacial phenomena.

Unlocking the Power of AI/ML for Smarter Materials Design

My research focuses on harnessing the power of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI/ML) in fields where data is scarce or costly.

To address the challenge of limited data, I’m developing tools that integrate:

  • Physico-chemical insights into material representations
  • Advanced training techniques like transfer learning and active learning to minimize the need for large labeled datasets

But it doesn’t stop there. I’m also interested in creating interpretable AI models that uncover structure–property relationships, making it easier to translate data into actionable insights for hypothesis-driven materials design.

I envision a future in which these tools unlock the potential of autonomous experimentation to accelerate the design cycle of functional materials.
